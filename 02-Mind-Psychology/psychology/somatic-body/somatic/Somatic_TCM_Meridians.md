# TCM & Meridians (中医经络与生命节律)

### The 5 Elements & Emotional Archetypes (五行与情绪原型)
| Element (五行) | Planet / Season | Associated Emotion (Neg/Pos) | Psychological Archetype | Professional Perspective (专业视角) | Citation (引用来源) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wood** (木) | Jupiter / Spring | Anger (怒) / Kindness (仁) | The Pioneer (The Visionary). | Governs the planning and execution of life vision; stagnation leads to frustration. | *Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon* (Huangdi Neijing). |
| **Fire** (火) | Mars / Summer | Mania (喜/亢奋) / Joy (礼) | The Wizard (The Charismatic). | Center of awareness and connection; imbalance results in anxiety/sleep issues. | Beinfield, H., & Korngold, E. (1991). *Between Heaven and Earth*. |
| **Earth** (土) | Saturn / Late Summer | Worry (思) / Trust (信) | The Nurturer (The Peacemaker). | Core of stability and mental digestion; overthinking dams the flow of energy. | Hammer, L. (1990). *Dragon Rises, Red Bird Flies*. |
| **Metal** (金) | Venus / Autumn | Grief (悲) / Integrity (义) | The Alchemist (The Perfectionist). | Deals with boundaries and letting go; excess leads to rigidity and isolation. | *The Web That Has No Weaver* (Kaptchuk, 2000). |
| **Water** (水) | Mercury / Winter | Fear (恐) / Wisdom (智) | The Philosopher (The Sage). | Source of fundamental vitality and willpower (Zhi); exhaustion leads to chronic fear. | *The Foundations of Chinese Medicine* (Maciocia, 2015). |

### 12 Meridians & Psychosomatic Connections (十二经络与身心连接)
| Meridian (经络) | Governing Function | Psycho-Emotional Theme | Somatic Sign of Imbalance | Professional Perspective (专业视角) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lung** (手太阴肺经) | Respiration; Boundary. | Grief, sadness, protection. | Chest tightness, asthma, skin issues. | The "Prime Minister" of Qi; manages how we interact with the outside world. |
| **Large Intestine** (手阳明大肠经) | Elimination; Letting go. | Inability to release the past. | Constipation, rigid thinking. | Physical and mental detoxification are inseparable in TCM theory. |
| **Stomach** (足阳明胃经) | Nutrition; Receiving. | Overthinking, anxiety, neediness. | Gastritis, appetite loss. | The first step in "digesting" both food and life experiences. |
| **Spleen** (足太阴脾经) | Transformation; Center. | Worry, brooding, obsession. | Fatigue, brain fog, heavy limbs. | Responsible for "Intellect" (Yi); excessive study or worry harms Spleen Qi. |
| **Heart** (手少阴心经) | Blood; Spirit (Shen). | Anxiety, insomnia, lack of joy. | Palpitations, restless sleep. | The "Emperor" of the body; if the Shen is unsettled, all other organs suffer. |
| **Small Intestine** (手太阳小肠经) | Sorting; Discernment. | Confusion, indecision. | Shoulder pain, lack of clarity. | Sorts "Pure" from "Impure" information (and nutrients). |
| **Bladder** (足太阳膀胱经) | Fluids; Nervous system. | Chronic stress, dread, over-working. | Sciatica, chronic back pain. | The longest meridian; acts as a protective shield against external stressors. |
| **Kidney** (足少阴肾经) | Jing (Essence); Vitality. | Deep fear, lack of willpower. | Lower back pain, premature aging. | The "Root of Life"; stores inherited energy and governs the response to fear. |
| **Pericardium** (手厥阴心包经) | Heart protector; Circulation. | Heartbreak, emotional armor. | Relationship difficulty, chest pain. | Closely linked to the Vagus nerve and emotional "Social Engagement" regulation. |
| **Triple Burner** (手少阳三焦经) | Energy distribution; Temp. | Fight or Flight; Hyper-vigilance. | Adrenal fatigue, temperature sensitivity. | Coordinates the HPA axis and overall metabolic/hormonal balance. |
| **Gallbladder** (足少阳胆经) | Decision making; Courage. | Indecision, suppressed anger. | Migraines (lateral), hip pain. | Provides the spark for action; "The General" who carries out orders. |
| **Liver** (足厥阴肝经) | Qi flow; Detox; Mood. | Anger, irritability, depression. | PMS, vision issues, tendonitis. | The organ most affected by modern stress and emotional stagnation. |

### Emotional First Aid Acupoints (情绪急救穴位)
| Point Name | Location | Function (功能) | Psychological Use Case | Professional Perspective (专业视角) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yintang** (印堂) | Between eyebrows. | Calms the Shen; anxiety. | Instant stress relief; insomnia. | Known as the "Extra Point"; corresponds to the Third Eye in other traditions. |
| **Neiguan** (PC6 - 内关) | Inner wrist, 3 fingers up. | Regulates Heart; nausea. | Panic attacks; heart palpitations. | Directly impacts the Vagus nerve; used clinically for anxiety-induced chest pain. |
| **Shenmen** (HT7 - 神门) | Wrist crease, pinky side. | Sedates the Spirit. | Severe anxiety; insomnia; mania. | The primary point for "Calming the Mind" and resolving sleep disturbances. |
| **Taichong** (LV3 - 太冲) | Foot, between 1st/2nd toe. | Moves Liver Qi; descends Fire. | Anger, frustration, PMS, stress. | The "Happy Point"; clears the pressure valve of repressed anger. |
| **Baihui** (GV20 - 百会) | Top of head, center. | Clears the head; lifts spirit. | Depression; dizziness; brain fog. | Meeting point of all Yang energy; used to "lift" sinking mood. |
