# 《我们讲故事是为了活着》We Tell Ourselves Stories in Order to Live

**作者**：琼·狄迪恩 (Joan Didion)  
**出版信息**：Everyman's Library, 2006  
**评分**：★★★★★ (5/5)

---

## 总体评价

《我们讲故事是为了活着》是一部全面收录琼·狄迪恩散文创作的权威选集，由著名的 Everyman's Library 于2006年出版。书名取自狄迪恩最著名的句子——出自《白色专辑》开篇的那句话："我们给自己讲故事，是为了活着。"这个标题本身就构成了一个完整的 poetics：它既是关于叙事的功能（我们为什么讲故事）、也是关于叙事的必要性（为了生存）、还是关于叙事的自欺（"给自己"讲述——不是客观 truth 而是 subjective construction）。

这部文集的价值首先在于它的 comprehensiveness。狄迪恩的散文生涯跨越四十余年、从1960年代到2000年代、涵盖文化批评、政治分析、旅行写作、个人回忆等多个 genre。这个选集将她的 major essays 汇聚一处、使读者能够追踪她作为散文家（essayist）的完整发展——从《向伯利恒跋涉》的 California cool 到《奇想之年》的 grief-stricken intensity、从六十年代的文化诊断到九十年代的政治解剖。

但对于关注女性生命叙事和关怀伦理的读者来说、这部文集具有特殊的意义。它提供了一种"纵向切面"——展示了狄迪恩如何在四十年间持续地、以不断演化的方式书写女性经验：作为女儿、作为妻子、作为母亲、作为 professional woman、作为 aging woman、as mourner。通过这些 essays 的并置阅读、我们可以追踪一个女性 intellectual 的生命轨迹、观察她如何在不同的 life stages 中 negotiate identity、career、relationship 和 loss。

## 核心内容

### 叙事的考古学

阅读这部文集的最重要方式之一是将其视为一部"叙事的考古学"——通过 layered reading 来追踪狄迪恩的 recurring themes 和 evolving perspectives。某些 images、sentences、even phrases 在不同年代的 essays 中 reappear、slightly modified、as if狄迪恩 were continuously revising and refining her own narrative of experience。

The most famous example is the opening sentence of《白色专辑》："我们给自己讲故事，是为了活着。"This sentence、in various forms、echoes throughout the collection——in the 1968 essays about the dissolution of sixties utopianism、in the 1970s essays about Hollywood and alienation、in the 1990s essays about political narrative、and finally in the 2000s essays about grief and mourning。Each repetition carries slightly different weight、depending on its context、creating a kind of musical variation on a theme。

For readers interested in women's life writing、this technique of self-quotation and revision is particularly significant。Women's narratives have historically been seen as"fragmentary"or"discontinuous"compared to the supposedly"coherent"narratives of male autobiography。狄迪恩's method turns this fragmentation into a strength：by allowing her essays to speak to each other across time、she creates a non-linear form of autobiography that is more faithful to the actual experience of female identity——which is less a linear progression than a continuous renegotiation。

### 从冷静到碎裂：声音的转变

One of the most striking effects of reading狄迪恩's essays chronologically is the gradual transformation of her narrative voice。The early essays——from《向伯利恒跋涉》——are characterized by a kind of crystalline detachment。The narrator is present but aloof、observing from a distance、maintaining ironic control。Even when writing about personal subjects（her migraines、her anxiety）、the tone is analytical rather than confessional。

This voice begins to shift in the 1970s and 1980s essays、particularly in the pieces about El Salvador and Miami。The political horrors she witnesses seem to crack the protective shell of her irony、allowing a more directly emotional tone to emerge。But the real transformation occurs in the late 1990s and 2000s、with the essays about her husband's death（《奇想之年》）and her daughter's death（《蓝夜》）。Here、the voice becomes fragmented、desperate、almost incoherent at times——as if grief had shattered the narrative competence that had been狄迪恩's signature。

This trajectory—from cool control to emotional fragmentation—can be read in multiple ways。One reading sees it as a narrative of decline、the loss of artistic control under the pressure of unbearable loss。Another reading、which I find more compelling、sees it as a narrative of deepening：狄迪恩's early detachment was not a strength but a defense、and her later fragmentation represents a courageous abandonment of that defense、an willingness to risk incoherence in pursuit of truth。In this reading、the later essays are not lesser works than the early ones but greater ones—achievements of a different order、testaments to the power of writing to survive even the most catastrophic losses。

### 女性的多重角色

Read as a whole、this collection offers a remarkable portrait of a woman negotiating multiple roles across a lifetime。We see狄迪恩 as daughter、in the essays about her parents and her California childhood。We see her as wife、in the essays about her marriage to John Gregory Dunne and their collaborative writing life。We see her as mother、in the essays about her daughter Quintana Roo。We see her as professional woman、in the essays about her career as a journalist and screenwriter。And finally、we see her as widow and bereaved mother、in the essays that confront the deaths of husband and child。

What is remarkable is how these roles are never presented as separate or competing identities。狄迪恩's essays show how the personal and the professional、the domestic and the public、the emotional and the intellectual、are constantly interpenetrating。She writes about politics as a mother、about motherhood as a writer、about writing as a form of survival。This refusal to compartmentalize is itself a feminist gesture——a rejection of the division between"women's concerns"and"serious subjects"that has historically marginalized women's voices in public discourse。

The collection also documents the aging of a woman intellectual——a subject rarely treated with seriousness in either literature or criticism。We see狄迪恩's concerns shift from the cultural dynamics of youth culture to the medical and existential challenges of aging。We see her body become increasingly present in her writing——not as object of vanity or shame but as site of experience、as instrument of perception、as limit and possibility。This embodied aging is particularly valuable for readers interested in how women writers navigate the transition from"visible"to"invisible"that so often accompanies female aging in contemporary culture。

### 关怀与见证的伦理

A theme that emerges with increasing urgency across the collected essays is what might be called an"ethic of witness"——a commitment to seeing and recording what is difficult、what is uncomfortable、what is conventionally excluded from polite discourse。狄迪恩 witnesses political violence in Central America、she witnesses the pathology of Hollywood culture、she witnesses the breakdown of American political discourse、and finally she witnesses her own grief and the death of those she loves。

This ethic of witness connects to broader concerns in feminist ethics and care ethics。Feminist theorists（如Carol Gilligan、Nel Noddings）have emphasized the importance of"attention"in ethical relationship——the capacity to perceive the other's needs、to respond to their vulnerability、to remain present in the face of suffering。狄迪恩's writing embodies this ethical attention in literary form。She does not look away from what is painful；she does not simplify what is complex；she does not offer easy comfort where genuine comfort is impossible。

The final essays in the collection、on grief and mourning、are perhaps the most profound expression of this ethic。In writing about her husband's sudden death and her daughter's prolonged dying、狄迪恩 refused the standard narratives of"closure"and"healing。"She insisted that grief does not resolve、that loss does not diminish、that the dead do not"live on"in any meaningful sense。This refusal of comforting narrative was not nihilism but honesty——a determination to tell the truth about death and mourning、even when that truth is unbearable。

For readers in the fields of thanatology、palliative care、and grief studies、these essays are essential texts。They demonstrate how literary writing can serve as a form of"narrative medicine"——not by offering therapeutic resolution but by providing companionship in suffering、by showing that one's most isolating experiences can be shared through language。狄迪恩's grief writing does not"help"the reader in any conventional sense；but it does something more important：it testifies、it witnesses、it refuses to let death and loss disappear into silence。

## 阅读体验

Reading this collection straight through is an overwhelming experience——not because it is long（though it is substantial）but because of the cumulative emotional weight of狄迪恩's forty years of observation and reflection。By the time one reaches the final essays on grief、one has lived through decades of American history、traveled to Central America and Hawaii、observed Hollywood's dream factories and Washington's power corridors、and followed one woman's life from young wife and mother to aging widow and bereaved parent。

The experience is something like reading a great novel in which the protagonist happens to be the narrator and the"plot"is the history of late-twentieth-century America。But unlike a novel、there is no artful resolution、no satisfying conclusion。The collection ends with grief、with loss、with the acknowledgment that narrative itself cannot save us from mortality。This ending、while painful、is also strangely beautiful——a testament to the courage it takes to write honestly about the most difficult subjects、and to the power of language to create meaning even in the face of meaninglessness。

I was particularly struck by the experience of reading the political essays in the context of the personal essays。When one encounters狄迪恩's analysis of the 2000 election after reading her account of her husband's death、the political analysis takes on additional depth——one sees it not just as political commentary but as an attempt to make sense of a world that has become incomprehensible through personal loss。Conversely、when one reads the grief essays after the political essays、one sees them as continuous with her lifelong project of bearing witness to a world in crisis、only now the crisis has become personal as well as political。

The collection also invites comparison with other major works of collected prose by women writers——Virginia Woolf's collected essays、Adrienne Rich's prose、Susan Sontag's essays。In this company、狄迪恩 holds her own、offering a body of work that is comparable in range、depth、and literary achievement。What distinguishes her is perhaps her commitment to a particular voice——that cool、precise、ironic California voice that remains recognizable across four decades of writing。This consistency of voice、combined with the evolution of its emotional register、creates a unique reading experience that is both familiar and surprising、comforting and disturbing。

## 评分

★★★★★ (5/5)

《我们讲故事是为了活着》is an essential collection、not just for狄迪恩 scholars but for anyone interested in twentieth-century American literature、nonfiction writing、and women's life narratives。The Everyman's Library edition is beautifully produced、with excellent editorial apparatus and a comprehensive selection that allows readers to trace狄迪恩's development across her entire career。This is a book to own、to read、and to return to——a genuine classic that will continue to reward rereading for generations。

For readers specifically interested in life narrative、care ethics、and women's writing、this collection offers an unparalleled resource。It documents one woman's negotiation of identity、career、relationship、and loss across a lifetime、showing how the personal and the political are always intertwined、how the intellectual and the emotional are always connected、and how writing itself can serve as a form of survival and a practice of care。狄迪恩's final message、delivered through the accumulated weight of forty years of essays、is that we do indeed tell ourselves stories in order to live——but that these stories must be honest ones、must face the darkness as well as the light、must acknowledge loss as well as celebrate connection。

This is not an easy book、nor a comforting one。But it is a true book、in the deepest sense of that word、and its truth is a gift to every reader who encounters it。I give it five stars without reservation、as a masterpiece of American prose、a landmark in women's writing、and a testament to the enduring power of the essay as a literary form。In the canon of American nonfiction、狄迪恩 occupies a place comparable to Thoreau、Emerson、and Twain；in the canon of women's literature、she stands with Woolf、Cather、and Morrison。This collection is the definitive statement of her achievement、and an invitation to future readers to discover why her voice remains so necessary、so urgent、so irreplaceable。
