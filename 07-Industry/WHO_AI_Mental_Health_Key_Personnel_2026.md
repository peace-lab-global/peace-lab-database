# WHO AI Mental Health Policy: Key Personnel Profiles

**Date**: 2026-07-09
**Subject**: Research profiles of Sameer Pujari (WHO AI Lead) and Dr. Kenneth Carswell (WHO NCD Mental Health)
**Context**: Both officials are central to WHO's emerging AI-for-mental-health policy framework, particularly the March 2026 expert consultation and the Global Initiative on AI for Health (GI-AI4H).

---

## 1. Sameer Pujari — AI Lead, WHO Digital Health & Innovation

### Background & Career

- **Current Title**: Lead AI, Digital Health and Innovation, World Health Organization (Geneva HQ)
- **Also**: Vice-Chair, WHO-ITU Joint Committee on Medical AI
- **WHO tenure**: Since early 2008 (18+ years at Geneva headquarters)
- **Core mandate**: Guided the formulation and diplomatic negotiations across all 194 WHO member nations for the WHO Global Digital Health Strategy
- **Oversight**: Supervises all AI projects across WHO
- **Focus areas**: Mobile health (mHealth), big data, machine learning, digital health governance
- **Field experience**: Provided on-the-ground technical assistance in 75+ countries
- **Publications**: Contributed to nearly 100 WHO reports and publications
- **Global Initiative on AI for Health (GI-AI4H)**: Key architect of this WHO initiative aimed at advancing AI governance across the United Nations system (published in *Nature*, 2026)

### Awards & Recognition

- **2016**: WHO Director-General's Excellence Award
- **2018**: Greenpeace Innovation Prize

### Prior Work

- Managed the WHO "Be Healthy, Be Mobile" project (mHealth initiative for NCD prevention)
- Spoke at AI for Good (ITU), Harvard Global Health, Digital Health Sans Frontieres, and other major forums

### 2026 Statements & Activities

#### Delft Digital Ethics Centre Workshop (January 2026)
*Context: Virtual expert meeting ahead of the India AI Impact Summit, 30+ global specialists in ethics, policy, psychology, and technology.*

> **"The pace of AI adoption in people's daily lives has far outstripped investment in understanding its impact."**
> — Sameer Pujari, WHO AI Director

This statement framed the workshop's central concern: that generative AI tools for emotional support—especially among youth—are being deployed faster than independent research can evaluate their psychological effects.

#### WHO Expert Consultation on AI for Mental Health (March 2026)
*Context: Published 20 March 2026, this landmark consultation brought together 30+ experts to chart WHO's way forward on responsible AI in mental health.*

Pujari participated alongside Dr. Alain Labrique (WHO Data & Digital Health Director) and Dr. Kenneth Carswell, contributing to three principal recommendations:

1. **Treat generative AI as a public mental health issue** requiring broad governmental and industry responses
2. **Incorporate psychological evaluations into digital monitoring** to track emotional reliance and other health determinants
3. **Develop supportive digital tools collaboratively** with clinicians and individuals with lived experience, ensuring cultural adaptation and evidence-based foundations

#### Global Initiative on AI for Health (GI-AI4H)
Pujari posted on LinkedIn about the GI-AI4H, emphasizing responsible AI governance, data standards, and health AI frameworks. The initiative was also published in *Nature* in 2026, outlining strategic priorities for advancing AI governance across the UN system.

#### Harvard Global Health Event (May 2026)
*Context: "Can AI Transform Global Health? Promise, Progress, and Reality" — a virtual seminar hosted by Harvard's global health division and medical school bioethics center.*

Pujari participated in discussions evaluating how machine learning can address systemic healthcare issues (staff deficits, diagnostic delays) while addressing the gap between donor aspirations and frontline realities.

## 2. Dr. Kenneth Carswell — Mental Health Specialist, WHO NCD & Mental Health

### Background & Career

- **Current Title**: Technical Officer, WHO Mental Health and Substance Use Branch (Noncommunicable Diseases / Mental Health)
- **Training**: UK-trained Clinical Psychologist
- **Based**: Geneva, Switzerland
- **Core mandate**: Directs WHO strategies on AI and online platforms for mental health; leads scalable psychological intervention development
- **Recognition**: Known for work that "turns simple, scalable tools into real-world mental health care"
- **World Economic Forum**: Listed participant/contributor
- **AI for Good (ITU)**: Featured speaker
- **ResearchGate/Google Scholar**: Active publication record in digital mental health interventions

### Key Projects & Innovations

| Project | Description | Impact |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Step-by-Step** | Narrative-based depression resource | Validated in large-scale RCTs; adopted by national medical networks in multiple countries |
| **Doing What Matters (DWM)** | Stress management app | Evaluated during the COVID-19 pandemic for scalability |
| **STARS** (Scalable Technology for Adolescents and youth to Reduce Stress) | Non-AI conversational chatbot for teenagers | Positive results in RCT in Jordan; published in JMIR Mental Health (2025) |
| **mhGAP Mobile** | Mobile version of WHO's Mental Health Gap Action Programme guide | Deployed to support medical staff in Nigeria and Nepal |
| **Self-Help Plus (SH+)** | Group-based stress management intervention | Expanded in Ukraine during the war; adult population |

### Awards & Recognition

- **2025**: eMHIC Global Impact Award — for equitable and evidence-based contributions to remote psychological support

### 2026 Statements & Activities

#### Delft Digital Ethics Centre Workshop (January 2026)

> **"WHO is committed to ensuring that users' well-being stays at the centre as these tools evolve."**
> — Dr. Kenneth Carswell, WHO Mental Health Specialist

This statement positioned WHO's stance on AI mental health tools: user welfare must remain central as generative AI tools for emotional support proliferate.

#### TU Delft Workshop (January 2026)

> **"We need research for this to help inform the decisions of key stakeholders."**
> — Dr. Kenneth Carswell, WHO

Carswell stressed the evidence gap — policymakers need rigorous research on AI's psychological effects before regulatory frameworks can be properly calibrated.

#### WHO Expert Consultation on AI for Mental Health (March 2026)
Carswell was a key WHO representative from the mental health side at the landmark consultation. He worked alongside Pujari (AI/digital health side) and Dr. Alain Labrique (data/digital health director), contributing the clinical and mental health policy perspective.

His dual emphasis throughout 2026:
1. **Evidence generation**: Independent research on AI's mental health impacts, particularly for youth
2. **User-centred design**: Digital mental health tools must be co-developed with clinicians and people with lived experience

#### Frontiers in Digital Health Publication (2025)
Carswell co-authored "The development of a World Health Organization transdiagnostic..." paper on scalable digital psychological interventions, published in *Frontiers in Digital Health* — directly relevant to his 2026 AI policy positions.

## 3. Joint Analysis: The Pujari-Carswell Policy Axis

### Complementary Roles

| Dimension | Pujari (AI/Digital Health) | Carswell (NCD/Mental Health) |
|-----------|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Domain** | AI governance, digital health infrastructure | Clinical psychology, scalable interventions |
| **Lens** | Technology policy & standards | Evidence-based mental health practice |
| **Focus** | System-wide AI oversight | User welfare & clinical validation |
| **2026 message** | "AI adoption has outstripped research investment" | "User well-being must stay central; we need research to inform policy" |
| **Approach to AI** | Governance-first: standards, accountability, oversight | Evidence-first: RCTs, clinical validation, co-design |

### Convergence Points in 2026

1. **Both advocate treating generative AI as a public mental health concern** — not merely a technology regulation issue
2. **Both emphasize the evidence gap** — AI tools for emotional support are deployed faster than they can be evaluated
3. **Both participated in the same January 2026 Delft workshop and March 2026 WHO expert consultation** — suggesting a coordinated WHO position
4. **The three WHO recommendations from March 2026** reflect both perspectives: governance structures (Pujari's domain) + psychological evaluation and co-design with clinicians (Carswell's domain)

### Policy Implications

The Pujari-Carswell axis represents WHO's dual-track approach:
- **Track 1 (Pujari)**: Top-down AI governance via the GI-AI4H, WHO-ITU joint committees, and digital health strategy across 194 member states
- **Track 2 (Carswell)**: Bottom-up evidence generation for scalable mental health interventions, with AI tools subjected to the same clinical validation standards as traditional interventions

Their combined 2026 messaging signals that WHO is moving toward:
- Classifying AI emotional support tools under public mental health frameworks (not just tech regulation)
- Requiring psychological impact assessments as part of digital health monitoring
- Building a global academic network to help countries adopt AI mental health tools responsibly
